Quick facts

  • Organizing body: Row Nova Scotia
  • Format: Coastal Beach Sprints (World Rowing Rules of Racing)
  • Boats: OC supplied (Row NS + Lite Boat)
  • Oars: Bring your own
  • Safety: Lifeguards on site + safety boats on water
  • Travel: Halifax Stanfield International Airport (YHZ) is the nearest major airport
Heads-up: Atlantic conditions can change quickly — plan for sun, wind, and cool water. (Translation: pack layers… and maybe a sense of humour.)

About the regatta

The Nova Scotia Invitational Beach Sprints Regatta brings together athletes, coaches, and officials to compete in a high-energy coastal sprint format. The event supports operational readiness for future championship-level events in Nova Scotia and will be delivered in alignment with World Rowing Coastal Beach Sprint Rules of Racing.

Eligibility

Participation is open to athletes entered by their respective rowing federations or clubs. Final eligibility and administrative requirements will be confirmed in Bulletin #2.

Team size

There is no formal team size cap for this invitational event. Entry limits may apply based on equipment availability and scheduling.

Boat allocation, rotation procedures, and any equipment briefings will be issued in Bulletin #2.

Venue

Rissers Beach Provincial Park on Nova Scotia’s South Shore offers a wide sandy beach and authentic Atlantic conditions — ideal for coastal sprint racing and operational testing.

Typical late‑August conditions

  • Water: cool-to-mild Atlantic water (often in the high teens °C)
  • Air: warm summer days with coastal variability
  • Wind & waves: variable; teams should plan for changing conditions
Conditions are always weather-dependent. Bring a plan for both “summer postcard” and “classic Atlantic character.”

Safety

On-site coverage

  • Lifeguards will be on site for the duration of competition.
  • Safety boats will be deployed in accordance with the Rules of Racing.

What to bring

  • Weather-ready layers (wind happens).
  • Hydration + sun protection.
  • Warm change of clothes for after racing.

Final safety plan details (zones, procedures, and communications) will be distributed closer to the event and/or in Bulletin #2.
